About Me

I'm a PhD student at Ghent University since 2013 advised by Chris Develder and Thomas Demeester, I am a member of IDlab.

My interests lie in the fields of natural language processing and information extraction. In the past I worked on topic models, keyphrase extraction, knowledge base population and statistical and neural machine translation.

In January 2016, I had the opportunity to visit the University of North Texas and work with prof. Cornelia Caragea on keyphrase extraction from noisy labeled documents. This stay was supported by a Travel Grant for a short stay by the FWO.

From February to April 2017, I was a research visitor at the Machine Intelligence Lab at Cambridge University to work on statistical and neural machine translation for text simplification and automated annotation. This stay was supported by a Travel Grant for a long stay abroad by the FWO.
